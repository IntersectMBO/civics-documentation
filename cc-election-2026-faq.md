# CC Election 2026 - FAQ

### 🧭 About the Election

<details>

<summary><strong>What is the Constitutional Committee (CC)?</strong></summary>

The Constitutional Committee is a governance body responsible for assessing whether governance actions comply with the Cardano Constitution.

It acts as a safeguard to ensure that decisions made through on-chain governance adhere to constitutional rules and principles.

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>What is the purpose of the CC election?</strong></summary>

The election allows the community, through Delegated Representatives (DReps), to select candidates to serve as members of the Constitutional Committee.

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>Does this election directly appoint CC members?</strong></summary>

No.

The election determines the preferred candidates. These candidates are then included in an Update Committee governance action, which must be ratified on-chain before they formally assume their roles.

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>Who can participate in the election?</strong></summary>

* **Candidates**: Individuals, organisations, or consortia
* **Voters**: DReps (stake-weighted voting)
* **Community members**: Can review candidates and participate in discussions

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>Is participation mandatory?</strong></summary>

No. Participation is voluntary for both candidates and voters.

</details>

***

### 👤 Candidates & Eligibility

<details>

<summary><strong>Who can run as a candidate?</strong></summary>

Anyone who meets the eligibility requirements defined by the Constitution and completes the registration process.

There are three tracks:

* Individual
* Organisation
* Consortium

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>What is required to register as a candidate?</strong></summary>

All candidates must provide:

* A cold credential (key or script)
* Contributions to Cardano
* Motivation
* Views on decentralised governance
* Constitutional familiarity and interpretation
* Transparency approach

Additional requirements apply depending on the selected track.

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>Is candidate information public?</strong></summary>

Yes, most of it.

**Public:**

* Name or alias
* Biography and governance statements
* Contributions and experience
* Social links
* Cold credential hash

**Private:**

* Contact email
* Declaration checkboxes

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>Can candidates edit their application?</strong></summary>

Yes, until the voting period begins.

Once voting starts, profiles are locked.

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>Can a candidate withdraw?</strong></summary>

Yes.

If a top candidate withdraws before the final governance action is submitted, the next ranked candidate may be selected.

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>Is the CC role compensated?</strong></summary>

The role is currently **uncompensated**, and future compensation is yet to be determined.

</details>

***

### 🔐 Cold Credentials (Critical Requirement)

> ⚠️ **Important:** Cold credentials are mandatory for all candidates.

***

<details>

<summary><strong>What is a cold credential?</strong></summary>

A cold credential is an on-chain identifier (key or script) used to authorise governance actions.

It represents the authority a Constitutional Committee member uses when voting.

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>Why are cold credentials mandatory?</strong></summary>

Because the role requires immediate operational capability.

CC members must actively participate in governance decisions. Without a valid credential, they cannot perform their duties.

This requirement ensures:

* Candidates are technically capable
* No delays after election
* Immediate participation in governance
* Seamless transition between members
* Continuity of the Constitutional Committee

It also prevents a critical failure scenario: a candidate is elected but cannot serve.

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>Why must credentials be prepared in advance?</strong></summary>

Credentials are verified before approval to ensure all candidates are:

* Genuine
* Prepared
* Able to act if elected

This avoids operational risks and removes uncertainty for voters.

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>Can credentials be changed after submission?</strong></summary>

Credentials are expected to remain stable once submitted and verified.

Any changes may require re-verification and must follow platform rules.

</details>

***

### 🗳️ Voting

<details>

<summary><strong>Who can vote?</strong></summary>

Only Delegated Representatives (DReps).

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>How does voting work?</strong></summary>

* Stake-weighted voting
* Each DRep may vote for up to three candidates
* Conducted on Intersect’s Hydra-based voting platform

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>How is voting power determined?</strong></summary>

Voting power is based on the amount of ada delegated to each DRep.

A snapshot is taken at the end of the voting period to determine final voting weight.

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>How are winners selected?</strong></summary>

Candidates are ranked by total stake-weighted support.

The top candidates are selected and included in the Update Committee governance action.

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>Can DReps change their vote?</strong></summary>

This depends on platform implementation, but final results are determined at the snapshot taken at the end of the voting period.

</details>

***

### ⚙️ Election Process & Timeline

<details>

<summary><strong>What are the phases of the election?</strong></summary>

1. Registration
2. Promotion
3. Voting
4. Results and audit
5. On-chain submission

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>What is the timeline?</strong></summary>

* **May 1 – May 31** → Registration
* **June 1 – June 13** → Promotion
* **June 13 – July 13** → Voting
* **August 1** → Update Committee governance action submission

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>What happens after the election?</strong></summary>

* Results are finalised
* Governance action is submitted
* DReps and SPOs vote on-chain
* If ratified, new members take office

</details>

***

### 🏛️ Roles & Governance

<details>

<summary><strong>What is Intersect’s role?</strong></summary>

Intersect facilitates the process by:

* Providing the voting platform
* Managing registration
* Verifying applications
* Coordinating execution

Intersect does not control the outcome.

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>What is the role of the Civics Committee?</strong></summary>

Provides oversight, including:

* Reviewing flagged applications
* Ensuring process integrity
* Supporting fairness and transparency

</details>

***

### 🛡️ Integrity & Safeguards

<details>

<summary><strong>How is the integrity of the election ensured?</strong></summary>

* Mandatory credential verification
* Public candidate profiles
* Stake-weighted voting
* Profile lock during voting
* Independent audit
* Separation between facilitation and decision-making

</details>

***

<details>

<summary><strong>Can applications be rejected or hidden?</strong></summary>

Yes.

If an application is incomplete, not genuine, or violates terms:

* Intersect may contact the applicant
* If unresolved, the Civics Committee may hide the application

Applications are not deleted, and decisions are documented publicly.

</details>
